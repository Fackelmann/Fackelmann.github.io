A few days ago it was news in Spain how the government had purchased some kits with a supposed sensitiviy of 90%. When they checked the first batch in their laboratories, it turns out that the sensitivity appeared to be only 30%.

I was vaguely familiar with this term from my undergrad. But as I was reading the article, I realized I didn’t really know what it meant. And what a better opportunity to learn!

In this entry I want to examine the most relevant measures of a test effectivenes. In the process I will also try to make it easily understandable, and not just a bunch of formulas. Finally, I will explain why these measure are useful in fields such as machine learning.

Hopefully this will be useful. It seems that these are concepts that many people have trouble with. Including the author of said article. There we can read:

Such a low sensitivity means that when the test is negative it is not possible to know whether it is a true negative or a false negative. This is, the patient could actually have the disease.

[1]

As you will see by the end of this entry, the author seems to be confusing Negative Predictive Value (NPV) with Sensitivity.

Basics (?)

Let’s first define some terms that will be used to define in a frequentist way.

  • True Positive, TP: A positive case labeled as positive.
  • False Positive, FP: A negative case labeled as negative.
  • True Negative, TN: A negative case labeled as negative.
  • False negative, FN : A negative case labeled as positive.

Sensitivity An Specificity

The sensibility of a test or, more generally, a binary classifier, is defined as the probability of the thest to correctly identify the positive cases [2]

\(Sensitivity=\frac{TP}{TP+FN}\)

We can also express it as a conditional probability. Let’s say that test represents a positive result in the test, and A represents the sample actually being positive. In that case:

\(P(test|A) = \frac{P(A|test)P(test)}{P(A)}\)

In a complementary way, the specifity of a test is defined as the probability of correctly identifying those patients who are negative.

\(Specificity=\frac{TN}{TN+FP}\)

It can also be expressed as:

\(P(\bar{test}|\bar{A})=\frac{P(\bar{A}|\bar{test})P(\bar{test})}{P(\bar{A})}\)
